✅ Full Adder using Half Adders

Instead of writing a full adder from scratch with raw gates, we reuse two already-built Half Adder modules and connect them with one extra OR gate.

  • Inputs: A, B, Cin
  • First half adder takes A and B → produces intermediate Sum (W1) and Carry (W2).
  • Second half adder takes W1 and Cin → produces final Sum and another carry W3.
  • Final Carry-out = OR of W2 and W3.
module full_adder_hier (input a, b, cin, output sum, cout); wire w1, w2, w3; half_adder ha1 (.a(a), .b(b), .sum(w1), .cout(w2)); half_adder ha2 (.a(w1), .b(cin), .sum(sum),.cout(w3)); or o1 (cout, w2, w3); endmodule

★ This is the classic example of hierarchical design — build once, reuse everywhere.

✅ Half Subtractor

Computes Difference and Borrow for two single-bit inputs using just XOR, AND, and NOT gates.

  • Difference = A XOR B
  • Borrow = (NOT A) AND B
module half_subtractor (input a, b, output diff, bout); wire a_n; not n1 (a_n, a); xor x1 (diff, a, b); and a1 (bout, a_n, b); endmodule

✅ Full Subtractor

Handles subtraction with a borrow-in too — needs 2 XOR, 2 AND, 2 NOT, and 1 OR gate, with 5 internal wires connecting everything.

module full_subtractor (input a, b, bin, output diff, bout); wire w1, w2, w3, w4, w5; xor x1 (w1, a, b); xor x2 (diff, w1, bin); not n1 (w2, a); and a1 (w3, w2, b); not n2 (w4, w1); and a2 (w5, w4, bin); or o1 (bout, w3, w5); endmodule

★ Notice the structural similarity to the Full Adder — same gate count pattern, just with extra NOT gates to handle borrow logic instead of carry logic.

✅ Important Concepts & Tips
🔹 Hierarchy

Reusing previously defined modules (like Half Adder) inside bigger designs (Full Adder) avoids redundant code, improves readability, and makes designs easier to debug and maintain.

🔹 Tool Usage

Use Elaborated Schematics in the design tool to visually confirm gate-level connections and verify that the module hierarchy is wired up correctly before simulation.

✅ Extra Notes — Universal Gates
  • NAND and NOR are called universal gates — any logic function (AND, OR, NOT, XOR, etc.) can be built using only one of them.
  • This is why the practice assignment is valuable: it builds intuition for gate minimization, important in real ASIC/FPGA standard-cell libraries.
  • Quick conversions: NOT(A) = NAND(A,A), AND(A,B) = NOT(NAND(A,B)), OR(A,B) = NAND(NOT A, NOT B).
  • Comparing Full Adder vs Full Subtractor: both use the same XOR-based core for Sum/Difference — the only structural difference is how Carry vs Borrow logic is generated.
